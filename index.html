Mathematical Disease Model

ODE System & Parameter Analysis

R/deSolve ODEs Epidemiology Policy Impact

Lyme Disease Transmission Dynamics

Comprehensive mathematical model using stage-structured ODEs to analyze white-footed mouse density effects on Lyme disease transmission. Features temperature-dependent parameters, real Montgomery County climate data, and quantitative policy recommendations.

View Research

Mathematical Framework: 9-equation ODE system modeling SIS mouse dynamics, stage-structured tick populations (larvae→nymphs→adults), and human infection accumulation with temperature-dependent parameters.

Key Finding: Critical threshold at 15 mice/acre above which human infections increase exponentially. Model predicts 12,400 cumulative cases over 2 years at peak density (40 mice/acre).

Research Impact: Provides quantitative evidence for ecological management strategies, demonstrating that reducing mouse density to 10-12 mice/acre could decrease human Lyme risk by 40-60%.

Actin Cytoskeleton Remodeling Research Proposal

Research proposal examining how the Rickettsia ricketsii proteins Sca4 and Sca2 remodel host cell cytoskeleton to promote infection. Using CRISPR-Cas9 engineered endothelial cells and live-cell imaging, I investigated how vinculin disruption enhances actin polymerization and bacterial dissemination.

Read Research Proposal

Research Question: How does R. rickettsii coordinate vinculin disruption and actin polymerization to facilitate bacterial dissemination between host cells?

Methodology: CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing to create vinculin-modified HMECs, live-cell fluorescence microscopy with mCherry-actin tracking, and quantitative bacterial motility analysis.

Innovation: Transforms a complex molecular biology research proposal into an engaging narrative that maintains scientific rigor while being accessible to diverse audiences.
